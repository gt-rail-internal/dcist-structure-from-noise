This HIT contains three parts, 15, 10 and 15 minutes, based on controlling and visualizing robots.


Each part will be presented to you in order and must be completed this way, click next when you are given the exit code from the previous stage.
You may take up to a 5 minute break in between each of the three parts but all three parts should be taken together in a single 1 hour time frame.

Have fun and good luck!
Instructions Part 1:
This part requires you to complete a task, for which you will be taken to another web page. Please read the instructions below carefully before attempting it.

Pressing keys to match patterns
When you start, you will be first asked for your Amazon Mechanical Turk ID in a pop-up (see image below). After entering your ID, click OK.


Click on the 'START' button. This will show you a pattern of 5 squares, and the web page should look like the image below. DO NOT click on the 'START' button again.

Each square corresponds to a key on your keyboard - 'Space', 'H', J', 'K' and 'L' - in that order from left to right (see image below).


Your task is to press the key corresponding to the square in red border as soon as the square pattern is displayed.
If there are two keys with a red border, you are required to press both keys simultaneously.
Click on the rectangular area with the squares before beginning to press keys.


Examples:

Correct key - space



Correct keys - H and L simultaneously

Instructions Part 3:
In this last part, you will control a team of virtual robots exploring three maps. Your goal is to have the robots explore the maps to find flags with information. Robots will download information from each flag, and once all information is obtained the flag will disappear.

This task will take you to another web page and has three phases, a tutorial map (2 minutes) and two regular maps where you must find 10 flags out of 15. Depending on how fast you can find the hidden flags, it is expected to take under 20 minutes total

Please read the instructions below carefully before attempting it.


1. Start
Copy your Worker ID before you begin.

When you go to the external webpage, you will be greeted by this as below. Zoom out your browser window (usually ctrl -) if needed to see the whole thing.



Click on the 'GO’' button, you will be asked for your Worker ID in a dialog box.


After entering your ID, click OK.
**Note if you go back to this page when the dialog box is open, your browser will crash. Refresh the page and try again.




Task Objective:
Your task will be to find flags dispersed around the map. They will be visible for 5 seconds during the beginning of the map phase for you to remember their positions. Flags will appear when you are nearby and collecting information from them. They will disappear once all information is collected and will not reappear or respawn.


Controls:
You will be able to select robots by click and dragging across them. Robots can be controlled whenever they are connected either directly or indirectly to the pentagon orange base on the top right. Disconnected agents can't be controlled.



Below the instruction box are five buttons (with associated keyboard shortcut letters) which will enable you to control the selected robots. If needed, an additional action click will be asked for the control. The button functionality are as below:


Set Heading (H): Robots will face at the same angle and move in the same direction as your additional action click.
Go To (G): Robots will move towards the target you select with your additional action click.
Repel From (F): Robots will move away from the target you select with your additional action click.
Random (R): Robots will move randomly.
Stop (S): Robots will stop.




Remember, you will be shown where the flags are in the beginning. Try to remember those positions as it will help you during the task.

To get credit for the task, you must engage with the task and find at least 10 robots out of the 15 shown in each of the 2 regular maps. The faster you can find the flags, the faster the activity will be completed!
You are required to complete the tasks in a single sitting, and will not be allowed to re-attempt it. Do not refresh your web page before completing it. The webpage will remind you of these instructions and tell you when to return here.
